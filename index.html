What is Computer Science?

Think of it as the art and science of teaching a computer how to think and solve problems. Computer scientists design and build software, create efficient algorithms, and explore new ways for people and computers to interact. From your smartphone apps to the complex systems running our economies, CS is the engine behind it all.

Alan Turing

The father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence.

Turing Machine: Foundation of computation theory
Turing Test: AI intelligence benchmark
Enigma Code: WWII codebreaking hero
Legacy: Turing Award named in his honor

Ada Lovelace

The world's first computer programmer and visionary.

First Algorithm: Program for Analytical Engine (1843)
Vision: Saw computers beyond just calculation
Innovation: Concept of loops and subroutines
Legacy: Programming language Ada named after her

Tim Berners-Lee

Inventor of the World Wide Web that connects us all.

World Wide Web: Created HTML, HTTP, URLs (1990)
First Website: info.cern.ch went live in 1991
Open Standard: Made the web free for everyone
Current: Director of W3C, web standards body

Linus Torvalds

Creator of Linux and Git, powering the digital world.

Linux Kernel: Powers billions of devices worldwide
Git: Version control system used by developers globally
Open Source: Champion of collaborative development
Impact: Android, servers, supercomputers all use Linux

Grace Hopper

Pioneer of programming languages and the term "computer bug".

First Compiler: A-0 System translated symbolic code
COBOL: Co-developed business programming language
"Computer Bug": Found actual moth in Mark II computer
Navy Career: Rear Admiral, served 42 years

John von Neumann

Architect of modern computer design and game theory.

Von Neumann Architecture: Foundation of modern computers
Game Theory: Mathematical framework for strategic decisions
EDVAC: First stored-program computer design
Legacy: Every computer today uses his architecture
